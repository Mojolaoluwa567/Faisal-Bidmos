FAAC Disburses N1.411 Trillion for October 2024

>Posted on 09 December, 2024

Prof. Uche Uwaleke, a capital market expert, highlighted critical issues with Nigeria's budget financing during a lecture on "The 2025 Proposed Budget and Capital Market Financing Options." He attributed a N5 trillion shortfall in capital expenditure for 2024 to inconsistencies in budgeting and reliance on unsuitable debt instruments. Key Points: Budget Concerns: Only N3.7 trillion out of N9.2 trillion for capital expenditure was utilized from January to August 2024, leaving a significant shortfall. The recurrent expenditure of N30 trillion, including non-developmental costs like car purchases, should rely on government revenue rather than borrowing. Debt Management Issues: The current domestic debt stock (N66.96 trillion) is misaligned with the Fiscal Responsibility Act of 2007, with 78% tied to FGN Bonds, not infrastructure-focused instruments like Sukuk and Green Bonds. Eurobonds make up 35% of external debt but account for 55% of debt servicing costs, raising concerns about their rising dominance. Recommendations: Utilize the capital market for capital expenditure funding while reserving government revenues for recurrent spending. Shift borrowing strategies to align with long-term, concessional financing, focusing on infrastructure development. Uwaleke emphasized the need for sustainable budget implementation and long-term economic strategies to manage Nigeria’s fiscal challenges effectively.
